I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the Civil and Environmental Engineering Department under the advisement of Professor Shaily Mahendra at University of California, Los Angeles. I'm also closely working with Professor Leonard H. Rome and Professor Junyoung Park at UCLA. Having worked at Professor Lisa Alvarez-Cohen's group, I received my M.S. from UC Berkeley in 2019. Before that, I received my Bachelorâ€™s Degree from Harbin Insitute of Technology (HIT), supervised by Professor Nanqi Ren, Professor Shijie You, and Professor Defeng Xing.

My long-term research goal is to establish a biocatalysis platform to achieve both effective biodegradation and biosynthesis. I am interested in enzymatic biotechnology, protein engineering, and metabolomics. My research area include:

  • Fungal enzymatic bioremediation in emerging contaminants (e.g., per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances, synthetic dyes, pharmaceutical compounds)
  • Fungal enzyme immobilization by vault nanoparticles to enhance enzyme properties
  • Metabolomics in mycoremediation that understands transformation flux during biodegradation
  • Material science and protein engineering for enzyme immobilization
